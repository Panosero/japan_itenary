✈️ Airport to Tokyo Travel

• Narita Airport: 60-90 min to Tokyo by Narita Express or Skyliner

• Haneda Airport: 30-45 min to Tokyo by monorail or Keikyu Line

• To mesm Tokyo: Located in Shimbashi area, easy train access

• IC Card: Buy Suica/Pasmo at airport for local transport (¥1,000-2,000)

• Cash: Withdraw yen at 7-Eleven ATMs (most international-friendly)

• First Meal: Convenience store bento or airport restaurant are safe bets

🏯 Kyoto Welcome

🚗 Hakone → Kyoto Travel Guide

• Total Journey: 3 hours 15 minutes (hotel to Kyoto accommodation)

• Step 1: FUFU HAKONE → Hakone-Yumoto (15 min taxi/shuttle)

• Step 2: Hakone-Yumoto → Odawara (15 min local train)

• Step 3: Odawara → Kyoto (2h 15min Shinkansen Hikari)

• Luggage Tip: Use yamato takkyubin service to forward luggage

• Seat Reservations: Book window seats on Mount Fuji side (right side going to Kyoto)

• Platform Info: Odawara Station has clear English signage for Shinkansen

🚇 Kyoto ⇄ Nara Travel Guide

• Route: Kyoto Station → Nara Station (JR Nara Line)

• Journey Time: 45 minutes direct train

• Cost: ¥720 one way (covered by JR Pass)

• Frequency: Trains every 30 minutes

• Walking: Nara Park is 10-min walk from station

• Day Trip Tip: Return trains run until 11:30 PM

• Deer Feeding: Buy deer crackers (shika senbei) ¥200 for 8-10 pieces

• Todai-ji Tip: Entry fee ¥600, allow 2+ hours for visit

✈️ Kyoto → Kansai Airport Travel Guide

• Route: Kyoto Station → Kansai Airport (HARUKA Express)

• Journey Time: 75 minutes direct express

• Cost: ¥3,570 (covered by JR Pass if still valid)

• Departure Frequency: Every 30 minutes from Kyoto

• Check-in Time: Allow 2+ hours for international flights

• Luggage Limit: Most airlines: 23kg checked, 7kg carry-on

• Tax-Free Shopping: Available at airport - bring passport & receipts

• Reserve Cash: Keep ¥4,000+ for train fare & airport meals

Cultural Etiquette

• Bow when greeting - a slight nod is perfect

• Say "Itadakimasu" before eating

• Say "Gochisousama" after eating

• Remove shoes when you see others doing so

• Never tip - it's considered offensive!

• Keep voices low on trains

• Stand on the left on escalators (right in Osaka)
